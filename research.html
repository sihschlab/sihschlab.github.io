Research Areas

Our research focuses on intelligent IoT systems, ultra-reliable low-latency communications, and data-driven optimization for future wireless networks designed under real-world constraints.

Research Area

We study how intelligent decision-making can improve wireless communications and IoT systems especially under dynamic channels, massive connectivity, and real-time constraints.

Focus
Design intelligent IoT system using machine learning
Artificial intelligence especially machine learning can significantly improve the design and management of network and communications components by learning from massive traffic and context data. We are interested in combining IoT with AI to build systems that decide when to connect, how to communicate, and when to avoid unnecessary transmissions.
AI + IoT
As IoT expands toward a hyperconnected society, maintaining countless connections can become a burden, increasing energy use, interference, and new environmental costs. We explore how intelligence can enable “intelligent connections” and even intentional disconnections without sacrificing service quality.
Example intuition
A drone receives commands from a remote controller. If the wireless link is unstable, the drone can use past commands and current context to infer likely future commands. If confidence is high, some commands may not need to be sent saving energy, reducing interference, and freeing resources for others. At scale (thousands of drones), this becomes critical almost like “telepathy.”
Traffic learning Context inference Resource saving Scalable IoT
Ultra-reliable and/or low-latency communication for networked control
Ultra-reliable communication is a key enabler for wireless networked control systems (a variant of IoT), including intelligent transportation, disaster/pollution exploration, and safety/surveillance applications. These systems demand highly reliable delivery of control commands and sensing data under mobility and unstable networks.
URLLC
Messages can be short and low-rate, but exchanged frequently. Connection setup overhead (preambles, time sync, etc.) becomes a severe burden. We study new designs such as random access strategies, joint data/meta-data encoding, and short blocklength coding, alongside advanced receivers (signal processing and successive interference cancellation) to mitigate collisions.
Short packets Random access Collision resilience SIC receivers
Data-oriented design and optimization of massive & ultra-reliable wireless IoT
5G increased focus on IoT connectivity, especially URLLC and massive MTC (mMTC). Traditional statistical models become suboptimal as networks grow in complexity and heterogeneity. We develop data-oriented models and machine learning approaches to optimize wireless IoT operations.
5G/6G IoT
Data-oriented approaches can optimize control information exchange, reduce protocol overhead, and integrate communication protocols with data stream mining coming from IoT devices supporting massive connectivity and high reliability under real-world constraints.
